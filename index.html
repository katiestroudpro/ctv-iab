Supporting Connected TV (CTV)IAB Tech Lab solutions you can use now

Now that we have settled the critical CTV vs. OTT debate, let's talk about what Tech Lab and its members have been doing to better support the CTV world!

Why is CTV so important?

"The TV and digital worlds are converging." This is something we have talked about for a few years now. The IAB Tech Lab even started a TV Convergence Task Force to work with other industry groups on related topics. Evidence of this convergence is finally coming together in 2020as we see a number of stars align:


New Streaming Services

The launch of numerous new streaming services like Disney+, HBO Max, Peacock, AppleTV+, and others, even as existing streaming services (most notably Netflix, Hulu, etc) continue to grow—and usually at the expense of broadcast, cable, and satellite TV services.


Increased Consumption on CTV

Significant growth in consumption on CTV devices even before COVID-19 while stay-at-home practices further accelerated that growth.


CTV Device Development

The continued development of new CTV devices like Amazon Fire, Apple TV, Roku and now devices from AT&T TV, TiVo, Xfinity and others further increase ease of access to CTV content.


Shifting Advertising Practices

A desire for traditional TV advertising to be more like digital with better targeting, measurement and other capabilities—leading to development of technologies like addressable TV, ACR, and others, along with initiatives like ATSC 3.0 and Project OAR. Tech Standards for advertising on CTV


For this converged world to become a reality, and also to truly deliver on the promise of CTV—at scale—the industry needs technical standards and policies that enable interoperability, efficiency and communication. In this blog series, we explore the various aspects of video ad delivery in the CTV environment and the corresponding standards that are available now or are currently in development at the Tech Lab. With the number of relevant efforts, we break them down into six different functional areas.
